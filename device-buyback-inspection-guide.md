# Device Buyback Inspection Guide

This guide helps inspect used electronics before buyback, resale, or refurbishment.

## Basic Condition Check

- Check for cracks, dents, scratches, or missing parts
- Confirm the device powers on
- Check if the screen, keyboard, buttons, or ports work
- Look for signs of liquid damage
- Check if the device has been opened or repaired before

## Account and Lock Status

- Confirm the device is not locked to a personal account
- Check for passcodes, activation locks, or management locks
- Make sure the seller can remove accounts before sale
- Do not buy devices that cannot be properly verified

## Serial and Model Information

- Confirm the model number
- Check the storage size and specifications
- Match the device information with the physical device
- Record the serial number for business records when appropriate

## Battery and Charging

- Test charging
- Check battery health when available
- Look for swelling or overheating
- Test with a known-good charger or cable

## Final Notes

A proper inspection helps protect both the buyer and the seller. Devices should be checked carefully before purchase or resale.
